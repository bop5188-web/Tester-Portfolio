This portfolio

This case study is intentionally meta: it tracks how I built the site you are browsing now. Below are three renderings—each one a real step in how my work is presented online, and how I improved structure, visual language, and interaction with every pass.

First rendering — structure and proof of concept

I started with plain pages and a simple goal: prove I could ship a portfolio without leaning on a template. The first version was mostly boxes and defaults—readable, but it did not yet feel like me. Spacing was inconsistent, hierarchy was weak, and the jump from “home” to “work” felt like two different sites. The win was getting content in place and learning where the layout actually broke on small screens.

From the first pass to the second

Between renderings one and two I focused on a shared system instead of one-off styles. I locked in type scale, muted backgrounds, and a calmer grid so project pages and the work index felt related. I also rewrote the work hub as a real destination: short intros, consistent cards, and clear paths into each case study. The site still did not move much, but it finally read as one portfolio instead of a stack of drafts.

Second rendering — system and case-study flow

The second rendering is where the “case study” idea took shape. I aligned the home previews with the work grid, tightened navigation, and paid attention to focus states and labels so keyboard and screen-reader use stayed honest. Visuals were still lighter on motion, but the information architecture was stronger: you could scan all projects, open one, and return without getting lost.

From the second pass to the third (this site)

The jump to the current site was about depth and delight without sacrificing clarity. I added a hero moment with Spline, neon SVG traces on cards, and scroll affordances that match how I actually want people to move through projects. Snap-style stops and the project dock on the home header make long pages feel intentional rather than endless. I also kept iterating in the open—this page is part of the same codebase as the rest, which is the point: the portfolio is both the artifact and the story of how I build.

Third rendering — where you are now

What you see today is the third rendering: cohesive pastel surfaces, the orb-backed work index, scatter-style case cards on the home page, and shared chrome across About and project templates. Each layer built on the last—structure first, then system, then motion and polish—so every new version was a real improvement, not a full reset. I am still tuning copy and imagery, but the spine of the site finally matches the quality I want my work to sit inside.
